BuilderSuite ML was built by **working home builders solving problems on our own jobs**. Every workflow — from sending bid packages, to scheduling subs, to closing the books — exists because we hit the same problem on real projects and nothing on the market actually fixed it.

## Native accounting vs. QuickBooks sync

This is the single biggest practical difference, and it's worth its own section.

Buildertrend's financial story relies on **syncing to QuickBooks**. That means:

- A second subscription
- A second login for whoever does the books
- A sync that can drift, double-post, or break at month-end — exactly when you need clean books most
- Two systems of record to argue about when numbers don't tie out

BuilderSuite ML has **full double-entry construction accounting built in**:

- Accounts Payable (with AI bill capture, vendor credits, partial payments)
- Accounts Receivable (customer invoices, payments, deposits)
- Banking (write checks, make deposits, bank reconciliation to the cent)
- Job costing — every transaction is mapped to a project and cost code
- Closing periods that lock the books once you're done with a month
- Audit-ready Balance Sheet, P&L, A/P Aging, and project-scoped reports

It's not "lightweight accounting" or "QuickBooks Lite." It's the same double-entry foundation a real accountant expects, with construction-specific job costing on top. No sync, no drift, no extra subscription.

## AI bill capture: the workflow that actually saves hours

Most "AI" features in construction software are marketing. Ours isn't.

In BuilderSuite ML, you can drag and drop **hundreds of vendor invoices at once**. The AI extracts:

- Vendor name (and remembers it next time)
- Invoice number, date, due date
- Line items and totals
- Cost codes it has learned you use for that vendor

Then it **matches the bill to existing purchase orders** so your bookkeeper doesn't have to dig. They click "approve" and the bill posts to A/P, the GL, and job costs in one step.

If your team is still keying in vendor invoices by hand — or paying someone else to — this alone usually pays for the switch.

## Where Buildertrend may still be the right call

We're going to be straight with you: Buildertrend is a mature product with a deep feature set, and there are a few cases where it's still the safer pick:

- You have an existing QuickBooks setup with a long history and a CPA who insists on staying there.
- You need a feature surface area we haven't built yet (very large enterprise GC workflows, specific franchise integrations, etc.).
- Your team is already trained on Buildertrend and the switching cost outweighs the upside today.

If that's you, no hard feelings — keep doing what works. But for most small-to-mid home builders we talk to, the math swings the other way pretty fast.
